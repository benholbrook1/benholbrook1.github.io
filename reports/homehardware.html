S24 @ Home Hardware

For the summer of 2024, I had the opportunity to join the Retail Helpline at Home Hadrware as a co-op student. In this report I will reflect on my time at this Comapany and give an overview of some of the work that I accomplished during this term.

Employer

Home Hardware is a Canadian home improvement retailer that specializes in offering building supplies, hardware, tools, home decor, and outdoor equipment. It operates a network of independently owned and operated stores across Canada, serving homeowners, DIY enthusiasts, and professionals. The company is headquartered in St. Jacobs, Ontario, and was founded in 1964.

In terms of Computer Science, Home Hardware has a large Infrastructure and Security Team, they develop multiple of their own Point Of Sale applications along with other tools and applications used by their retail stores. They also have a large inventory management system to manage the logistics of how each store is supplied with their merchandise and products.

My Role

In my time at Home Hardware I was a part of the Retial Helpline team. In this role my team and I served as the first point of contact for any IT support needed from any of Home Hardware's 1100 retail stores. Some of my responsibilities in this role were:

  • Assisting Store Owners with the initial instalation of Home Hardware software and hardware products
  • Providing ongoing support for the use of these Home Hardware products
  • Research and documentation of store suggestions for enchancements in Home Hardware's software products
  • Assisting Home Hardware Stores with maintaining computer hardware and operating systems

Goals

  1. Familiarize myself with Home Hardware's tech stack

    2. In this role I was excited to get the chance to see how different applciations function and gain experience learning the framework of production type applications. This proved to be true as I was able to learn about the inner-workings of multiple different types of applications that we supported at the helpline. I worked with SQL based Point-Of-Sale applications and also andriod based inventory management software.

  2. Learn problem solving tecniques for approaching complex service tickets

    3. A goal of mine while at Home Hardware was to learn how to disect and approach a problem solving scenario to resolve service tickets efficiently. Throughout the summer I had the chance to face over 850 service tickets and work through the process of resolving each case. I learned the value in getting the context of the problem and ensuring I understood what the customer required, I learned how to apply critical thinking and make use of internal documentation to take steps in solving the problem and I learned how to reach out for help when a problem could be better serviced by another one of Home Hardware's internal teams.

  3. Effective Oral Communication

    4. In this role I gained a large amount of experience communicating with both technical and non-technical stakeholders. I learned how to be clear and consise in my communication and how to keep everyone looped in in all parts of the problem solving process. I learned how to work with difficutlt personalities and how to de-escilate scenarios where a store owner or manager might be upset.

Conclusion

Overall I am extremely grateful for my time at Home Hardware. I was able to be a part of a great team, who were all willing to step in and help with any questions I had. I had the chance to be a part of a very professional environment and learn different skills required to work effectively in the workplace. I enjoyed learning about the different software used at Home Hardware and will take this valueable knowledge into the other opportunities that I will have in the future. Looking back on the term it was a fulfilling and rewarding experience.
